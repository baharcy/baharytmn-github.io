RESEARCH METHODS

Demonstration of Projects, Reflections, and Other Artefacts: My Journey in Research Methods.

Unit 1

In this unit, we explored the scientific method, emphasising the distinction between deductive and inductive reasoning. To reinforce our comprehension, we completed a brief quiz on this topic. While the quiz seemed straightforward, it effectively highlighted the key difference: deductive reasoning starts with general premises and moves toward specific predictions, whereas inductive reasoning forms generalisations based on observations. Additionally, the unit delved into ethical considerations in AI and the importance of ethical standards, focusing on fairness, transparency, and privacy, with insights from Correa et al. (2023) and Deckard (2023). We also examined the ACM and BCS codes of conduct for computing professionals. Through our collaborative discussions, we analysed various case studies where computing professionals face ethical challenges, enhancing our understanding of how these principles apply in real-world scenarios. Moreover, we were provided with an outline of the entire module during our first Zoom session, which helped guide our learning throughout.

References:

Correa, N. K., Galvao, C., Santos, J. W., Del Pino, C., Pinto, E. P., Barbosa, C., ... & de Oliveira, N. (2023). Worldwide AI ethics: A review of 200 guidelines and recommendations for AI governance. Patterns, 4(10).

Deckard, R. (2023) What are Ethics in AI

Unit 3

Unit 5

The focus of the unit was on qualitative data collection methods, particularly interviews, survey methods, and questionnaire design. One key takeaway was the distinction between open-ended and closed-ended questions, along with the benefits and limitations of each. We also explored how using the wrong question types can negatively impact the quality of a questionnaire. Additionally, the chapter revisited concepts I am familiar with from previous modules, such as the definitions of population and sample. The introduction of pre- and post-testing concepts was especially enlightening. These methods are essential for evaluating and improving the clarity of surveys, ensuring that they effectively capture the necessary information. I now understand how these steps are crucial for creating a clear before-and-after visual, allowing researchers to assess the impact of changes in survey design. Most importantly, I gained a deeper understanding of how to analyse respondents' answers. I learnt that effective analysis requires careful attention to the structure of responses and how they align with the research objectives, which will be invaluable for my future research projects.
